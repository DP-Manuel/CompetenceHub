## Brand Foundation

The design should communicate professionalism, trust, stability, competence, reliability, honesty, future orientation, education, movement, innovation, social responsibility, humanity, helpfulness, openness, integration, flexibility, and people-centered work.

## Typography

- Primary typeface: Roboto.
- Use other fonts only for exceptional advertising-style headlines, not for the core website.
- Avoid heavy italic use.
- Text on colored backgrounds should be heavier for readability.

## Colors

- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Pantone 320: `#009ca6`
- Kaminrot: `#FF7F57`
- Dunkelgrau: `#333132`
- Gelb: `#FFB758`

## Color Usage

- Pantone 320 or Kaminrot should always be present.
- Use Pantone 320 and Kaminrot mainly for headings, backgrounds, boxes, lines, icons, arrows, and structural shapes.
- Do not use Pantone 320 as text on Kaminrot.
- Do not use Kaminrot as text on Pantone 320.
- Use dark gray for general text and headings.
- Use yellow sparingly for subheadings or strokes, especially on Pantone 320.
- Do not use yellow as text on white.

## Layout Motifs

- Primary shape: rectangle.
- Use rectangles as bars, boxes, lines, dividers, section anchors, and CTA framing.
- Secondary shapes: triangles for direction/future/movement; circles for people-centered imagery.
- Every core layout should include at least one small Pantone 320 rectangle or horizontal/vertical bar.
- Logo belongs top left with equal top and left spacing.
- Keep page margins consistent.
- Let Pantone elements or images break the margin intentionally when useful.
- Use the small rectangle on the right half of layouts when paired with the top-left logo.
- Keep text sparse and avoid overcrowding.

## Imagery

- People should be in the foreground.
- Use friendly, hopeful, bright, human images.
- Imagery should make the target group believe in a better future for themselves.
- Illustrations should be modern, minimalist, serious, and useful as eyecatchers.

## Website Design Direction

- Calm professional B2B site, not a playful marketing splash.
- Strong white space with dark gray text.
- Use Pantone 320 as the main recognition color and Kaminrot as an energetic complementary signal for CTAs or key process moments.
- Process diagrams should use rectangular steps and arrows/triangles rather than decorative abstract visuals.
- Candidate/participant imagery can be cropped in circles; company/process content should lean on rectangles and bars.
- Avoid text-heavy hero copy; make the first screen explain the offer through one direct headline, one support sentence, and a visible process cue.

## Media Designer Onepager Evidence

The Firmendingsbums onepager provides a concrete, professionally designed application of the corporate design and should be treated as a strong visual reference for the seminar-facing website sections.

- Confirmed palette: Pantone 320 teal as the dominant recognition color, Kaminrot/coral for topic headers and accents, yellow/orange for secondary lines and link cues, dark gray for body copy, and a pale blue-gray section background.
- Confirmed typography: large, bold teal headings; compact bold white headings on coral cards; restrained dark body copy with generous hierarchy.
- Confirmed layout language: strong horizontal teal/coral bars, rounded rectangular topic headers, white information cards, thin teal dividers, and a large framed CTA panel.
- Confirmed seminar taxonomy: `Führung & Mitarbeiterbindung`, `Vertrieb & Kundenkommunikation`, `Rhetorik & Konfliktmanagement`, and `Prävention & Resilienz`.
- Useful content depth: each seminar category contains six concrete topic examples. The website can expose these progressively instead of limiting each category to one abstract summary.
- Imagery direction: friendly, inclusive illustrations sit above the topic details. The current photographic seminar image can remain useful, but the seminar cards should visually align more closely with the onepager when approved illustration assets are available.
- CTA direction: use a direct consultation invitation and an individually tailored seminar offer as the conversion message. QR-code treatment is print-specific and should not be copied into the website.
- Privacy/publication guard: named contacts, direct email addresses, and phone numbers from the onepager require explicit confirmation before publication or reuse on the website.

## Recommended Website Application

1. Keep the current homepage narrative and exact CI color tokens.
2. Refine the seminar page into four richer topic cards using the onepager hierarchy and concrete bullet topics.
3. Reuse the onepager's stronger local positioning around practical company seminars in Würzburg.
4. Align the seminar CTA visually with the framed white/teal panel while preserving accessible contrast and responsive behavior.
5. Request original illustration/logo exports from the media designer before extracting or recreating assets from the PDF.
