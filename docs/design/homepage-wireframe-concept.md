# Homepage Wireframe Concept

Last updated: 2026-06-09

## Purpose

This document gives Manuel two homepage wireframe directions to compare with the team. Both variants use the same strategic foundation:

- Sub-brand with visible Donner + Partner endorsement.
- Primary audience: companies.
- Primary CTA: `Kontakt aufnehmen`.
- Real contact form section in the page concept.
- B2B-first messaging around seminars, needs discovery, qualification, and placement.
- Future platform/app content remains secondary and carefully framed.

## Shared Assumptions

- The public offer name is not final. Use placeholder: `[Submarke]`.
- Header endorsement: `[Submarke]` with small line `Ein Angebot von Donner + Partner`.
- Corporate design stays binding: Roboto, Pantone 320 `#009ca6`, Kaminrot `#FF7F57`, Dunkelgrau `#333132`, Gelb `#FFB758`, white base.
- Logo placement: top left.
- Contact form is designed now, but implementation requires privacy/legal approval before collecting data.

## Shared Navigation

Desktop:

- Start
- Für Unternehmen
- Seminare
- Vermittlung & Qualifizierung
- Branchen
- Kontakt
- CTA button: `Kontakt aufnehmen`

Mobile:

- Logo/sub-brand top left.
- Menu icon top right.
- CTA should remain visible in mobile menu and at key page sections.

## Variant A: Seriös-Corporate

### Intent

This version should feel stable, trustworthy, institutional, and easy to approve internally. It borrows more from ProPharma, Slack Healthcare, and Doximity: clear segmentation, compliance sensitivity, restrained visuals, and high trust.

Best when:

- The team wants low-risk corporate credibility.
- The site should feel close to Donner + Partner.
- Legal/privacy and institutional trust are central.

### Visual Character

- White background with precise Pantone 320 bars and thin dividers.
- Dark gray text.
- Kaminrot used sparingly for CTA and one process accent.
- Rectangular cards with low radius or sharp edges.
- Large human image, but calm and professional.
- Little motion; maybe subtle section reveals later.

### Section Notes

Hero:

- Strongest candidate headline: `Weiterbildung, Recruiting und Vermittlung in einem System`.
- Keep process cue visible in first viewport.
- Use the sub-brand as the big identity signal, not just tiny header text.

Trust strip:

- Place directly after the hero.
- Use existing measures as proof, not invented metrics.
- Possible labels: `Sprachkurse`, `Bewerbungscoaching`, `Aktivierung`, `FBW-Maßnahmen`.

Process:

- Clean numbered rectangular steps.
- The process should feel operational and credible, not decorative.

Contact form:

- Formal and clear.
- Privacy note visible before submit.
- No newsletter opt-in in first version.

### Strengths

- Highest credibility.
- Easy to explain to internal stakeholders.
- Strong alignment with the corporate design manual.
- Lower risk of looking like an unapproved startup spin-off.

### Risks

- Could feel too conservative if the sub-brand should signal innovation.
- Needs strong imagery or spacing so it does not become brochure-like.

## Variant B: Modern-Dynamic

### Intent

This version should feel more like a forward-looking sub-brand: clearer energy, more visual rhythm, stronger process storytelling, and a more memorable first impression. It borrows more from Miro, monday.com, Cedar, and Chainalysis, but translated into the Donner + Partner color and shape system.

Best when:

- The team wants the offer to feel new and distinct.
- The sub-brand should create its own recognition.
- The site should make the integrated model feel active and scalable.

### Visual Character

- White base, but stronger section bands.
- Pantone 320 used as bold blocks and process lanes.
- Kaminrot highlights the "Bedarf" and CTA moments.
- More asymmetrical layouts.
- Overlapping rectangles and directional triangle cues.
- People image plus abstract process layer, not product screenshots.

### Section Notes

Hero:

- Strongest candidate headline: `Aus Seminaren wird Personalperspektive`.
- This is more ownable and sub-brand-like than the corporate variant.
- Process cue becomes a visual object, not just a line of text.

Benefit strip:

- Replaces conservative proof strip with outcome language:
  - `Klarere Bedarfe`
  - `Passendere Profile`
  - `Kürzere Einarbeitung`

Process:

- More prominent, almost the visual backbone of the page.
- Kaminrot marks the turning point: company need becomes qualification input.

Digital roadmap:

- Variant B can carry the future platform/app better, but it must remain careful:
  - `Digital anschlussfähig geplant`
  - no fake UI screenshots
  - no "already available" wording

Contact form:

- Two-column closing section.
- Left side explains the low-friction next step.
- Right side contains the form.

### Strengths

- More memorable.
- Better for a distinct sub-brand.
- Makes the integrated model feel active and innovative.
- Stronger visual story for a team presentation.

### Risks

- Needs careful CI discipline so it does not drift away from Donner + Partner.
- Slightly higher risk of implying a more finished platform than currently exists.
- Requires good visual execution; weak imagery would hurt this variant more.

## Contact Form Wireframe

Use the same fields in both variants:

- `Name`
- `Unternehmen`
- `E-Mail`
- `Telefon`
- `Ich interessiere mich für`
  - `Firmenseminare`
  - `Personalbedarf`
  - `Qualifizierung & Vermittlung`
  - `Kooperation`
  - `Sonstiges`
- `Nachricht`
- Privacy checkbox:
  - `Ich habe die Datenschutzhinweise gelesen und stimme der Verarbeitung meiner Angaben zur Kontaktaufnahme zu.`
- Submit button:
  - `Anfrage senden`

Required:

- Name
- E-Mail
- Interest
- Privacy checkbox

Optional:

- Company
- Phone
- Message

Legal note:

- Final wording and form processing must be reviewed before implementation.
- Until privacy setup is approved, form can be implemented visually but disabled or replaced by a contact mail link.

## Mobile Wireframe Rules

Both variants:

- Header height should stay compact.
- Hero headline should fit in 2-4 lines.
- Process steps stack vertically.
- CTA appears after hero copy and again after process/service section.
- Form fields become single-column.
- Avoid sticky elements that cover content.
- Keep touch targets large enough and labels visible.

Variant A mobile:

- More document-like, calm stacking.
- Trust strip appears as two-column or horizontal scroll chips.

Variant B mobile:

- Process cue appears directly below hero CTA as stacked step chips.
- Use fewer decorative offsets to avoid cramped mobile layout.

## Team Comparison

| --- | --- | --- |
| Trust | Very high | High |
| Sub-brand distinctiveness | Medium | High |
| CI safety | Very high | Medium-high |
| Innovation signal | Medium | High |
| Legal/compliance comfort | Very high | High |
| Presentation impact | Medium | High |
| Implementation complexity | Lower | Medium |
| Risk of overpromising | Low | Medium |

## Recommendation

Use Variant B as the presentation favorite, but borrow Variant A's trust/compliance discipline.

Practical hybrid:

- Hero and process from Variant B.
- Trust strip and compliance section from Variant A.
- Service modules shared by both.
- Contact form from Variant B's two-column close, with Variant A's sober legal note.

This gives the sub-brand enough energy without losing Donner + Partner credibility.
