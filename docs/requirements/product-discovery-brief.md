# Product Discovery Brief

Last updated: 2026-06-09

## Sources

- `Quellen/Konzept Unternehmen.docx`
- `Quellen/Corporate Design Manual Stand 12.03.25.pdf`

## Mission

Build a website that explains and sells an integrated education, recruiting, qualification, and placement model. The first version should make the model understandable for companies and create trust for seminars, personnel needs discovery, and later placement cooperation.

The broader system vision includes a later internal web application and possibly a language-learning app that support recruiting, course management, matching, reporting, and coach/trainer workflows.

## Users And Stakeholders

- Primary website audience: small and medium-sized companies with training, personnel, or hiring needs.
- Secondary audience: jobseekers, career changers, and people with qualification needs.
- Internal stakeholders: project owner, sales/seminar team, trainers/coaches, coordination/matching roles, legal/privacy reviewers.
- Parent brand / corporate design stakeholder: Donner + Partner digital media / CI ownership.

## Current State

- The business model should grow without meaningful external investment.
- Costs should mainly be internal working time.
- Existing Donner + Partner courses and measures should be reused instead of rebuilt.
- The first digital artifact is the public website; later artifacts may include an internal platform and language-learning app.

## Target State

- Companies enter through seminars, build trust, and reveal personnel or qualification needs.
- Needs flow back into course and qualification content.
- Participants are qualified for concrete company requirements.
- Matching and placement become a repeatable process.
- Existing measures become an active recruiting and placement system.

## Core Model

- Area A: recruiting and placement in Germany.
- Area B: company seminars as an independent revenue and sales channel.
- Central process: seminar -> company contact -> personnel need -> placement -> follow-up qualification.
- Existing course base: integration and language courses, individual application coaching, language coaching, activation measures, and FBW measures in bottleneck professions.

## Website Implications

- The website should speak first to companies, not only to participants.
- The central promise should be practical: seminars create trust, reveal needs, and connect companies with qualified people.
- The first version should make the loop visible with a simple process section.
- Company calls to action should focus on seminar inquiry, personnel needs consultation, and cooperation contact.
- Participant content can exist as a secondary path, but should not dilute the B2B entry point.

## Candidate Website Sections

- Hero: concise promise for companies, with primary CTA for consultation or seminar inquiry.
- Problem: staffing gaps, onboarding friction, qualification mismatch.
- Solution: seminars + needs discovery + targeted qualification + placement.
- Process: seminar, analysis, qualification, matching, entry support.
- Seminar topics: sales, rhetoric, communication, leadership, employee retention.
- Target sectors: care and health, logistics and warehouse, construction/electrical/trades, IT and office/support.
- Existing strengths: existing measures, coaches, regional knowledge, low-investment scalable model.
- Future digital support: platform and language app as roadmap, not as first-version promise unless approved.
- Trust and compliance: legal/privacy review required before operational launch.
- Contact: clear B2B inquiry form or contact path.

## MVP Boundary

First website MVP:

- Public marketing website for the integrated model.
- B2B-first content and call to action.
- CI-aligned visual design.
- Static content architecture that can later connect to forms, CRM, platform, or app.
- No user accounts, candidate profiles, matching engine, or participant data processing in the first step unless explicitly scoped.

Non-goals for the first website MVP:

- Full internal platform.
- Language-learning app.
- Automated matching.
- Contract management.
- Candidate data storage.
- Operational workflows that require legal/privacy sign-off.

## Constraints

- Corporate font: Roboto.
- Corporate colors: Pantone 320, Kaminrot, white, dark gray, optional yellow accent.
- Layout should use rectangles/bars as primary visual forms.
- Logo should be placed top left with even spacing.
- Visual language should be human, hopeful, friendly, serious, and modern.
- Text should be concise; the corporate design manual recommends sparse text and uncluttered layouts.
- Legal review is mandatory before starting the operational model.
- DSGVO, platform access security, participant consent, and data sharing are core risks.

## Success Criteria

- Companies understand the offer within seconds.
- The process loop is clear without requiring a long explanation.
- The website generates qualified B2B conversations or seminar inquiries.
- CI rules are visibly respected.
- Later webapp/platform scope remains separated from first website scope.

## Risks And Mitigations

- Risk: The site tries to address companies, participants, internal stakeholders, app users, and legal topics equally.
- Mitigation: Make companies the primary path and keep participant/platform content secondary.

- Risk: Claims about placement, candidate data, or app functionality become legally sensitive too early.
- Mitigation: Use careful wording and mark operational flows as subject to legal/privacy setup.

- Risk: Design becomes generic despite strong CI.
- Mitigation: Use Pantone 320 and Kaminrot as structural layout elements, Roboto, top-left logo, rectangles/bars, and human imagery.
